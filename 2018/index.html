Annual Enrollment is your opportunity to enroll in McDonald’s health benefits, and/or make changes to your existing plans for 2019. After the Annual Enrollment period ends, you won’t be able to make changes or drop your coverage unless you have a qualified life event like a marriage or birth of a child.

Once you sign up, you will be required to make premium payments. If you don't make your premium payments, your coverage will be cancelled.

We’ve put together this guide to help you learn about and navigate your choices. Simply click on the tabs above.

If you take no action during Annual Enrollment, the chart below shows what happens

If you currently… Then you will…
Have McDonald’s medical, dental and/or vision coverage Remain in your current plans for 2019, including the same surcharges and dependent coverage
  • Because there are some changes to the plans in 2019 – including costs – it’s important that you consider at all your options
  • Spending accounts require re-enrollment each year
Do not have McDonald’s medical, dental and/or vision coverage Not have coverage in 2019
Have medical coverage through the public exchanges Not be eligible for a subsidy on that coverage through the public exchanges in 2019

Hawaii residents: If you choose not to enroll in a McDonald’s medical plan, you must complete the HC-5 form that will be mailed to your home and return it to the McDonald’s Health & Retirement Center by December 31, 2018.
