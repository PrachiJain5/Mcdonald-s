Review 2018 Premiums and Plan Changes

While there aren't a lot of big changes to Target benefits this year, the small changes will make a big difference for many of you and your families. Keep reading to see how free, increased, and lower cost can add up for you!

Premiums

open all | close all

Medical: Increasing 2-4%

Medical premiums are increasing from 2-4%, depending on the plan you choose.

  • For Team Member Only, this is approximately $1.78 - $2.64 per month
  • Less than the standard increases of 5-7% in the retail industry across the nation
  • No changes to deductibles or out-of-pocket maximum
Medical: Decrease

All team members who are currently paying a premium will see a decrease.

Dental: No change

Target is improving the dental benefits at no additional cost to you.

Vision: Decrease

You will pay less for the Vision Eyewear Plan that provides a $150 allowance for purchasing glasses and contacts from an in-network provider.

Medical/Prescription Drugs

open all | close all

Increased Infertility coverage

The lifetime maximums for infertility are increasing significantly:

  • Medical from $7,000 to $25,000
  • Prescription Drug from $3,500 to $5,000
More free generic preventive drugs

The number of free generic preventive drugs is expanding, including some for mental health. The list already includes generic preventive drugs for chronic conditions such as high cholesterol, hypertension, asthma and diabetes.

Lower cost for many specialty drugs in the HSA Plan

Today, you pay 35% for all specialty drugs. In 2018, you will pay less for specialty generic and specialty preferred brands. You will pay more for specialty non-preferred.

Required second opinion before planned back surgery

Team members enrolled in a UnitedHealthcare HRA or HSA plan must get a second opinion through Grand Rounds before planned back surgery or you will pay a $500 surgery fee for your surgery. Note: You are not required to follow the second opinion.

New Kaiser option for some team members

If you live in a location where the Kaiser HRA Plan is available, you now have the option of choosing the HSA Plan. It is identical to the UnitedHealthcare HSA Plan, but you access the Kaiser network.

Tobacco

open all | close all

Tobacco surcharge increase

The tobacco surcharge per smoker is increasing from $390 to $800. The maximum number of surcharges that can be applied to a household is still three: team member, spouse/domestic partner, and one charge for any number of other dependents age 18+ who indicate they use tobacco.

Short-term Disability, Maternity, and Paid Parental Leaves

open all | close all

Increased Short-term Disability (Non-exempt)
  • All eligible non-exempt team members will receive 75% pay replacement for days 1-60 and 58% for days 61-26 weeks.
  • All eligible non-exempt team members will receive 75% pay replacement for days 1-60 on approved Short-term Disability/Paid Medical leaves. For days 61-150, pay replacement will be 60%.
  • Non-exempt store team members will now be eligible for STD at 6 months and 20+ average weekly hours.
Increased Short-term Disability for Maternity (Non-exempt)
  • All eligible non-exempt team members will receive 100% Short-term Disability pay replacement for days 1-60 if out on an approved maternity leave.
Increased Paid parental leave (Non-exempt)
  • All non-exempt team members will be eligible for 2 weeks of paid parental leave at 100% pay replacement after 1 year of service and 20+ average weekly hours.

How Tobacco Use Affects Your Costs

Every year, all team members enrolling in benefits must indicate whether they and covered members of their families use tobacco or nicotine products. This includes tobacco/nicotine use in any form (including but not limited to e-cigarettes, vaping, and hookah).

If you don't share your tobacco use status, you will automatically default to a tobacco user. As a result, you'll pay the $800 tobacco surcharge for your medical plan and higher premiums for benefits including Long-term Disability, Supplemental Life Insurance and Critical Illness.

open all | close all

How many people in my household will pay the $800 tobacco surcharge?

The maximum amount a household would pay in tobacco surcharges is $2,400. The $800 tobacco surcharge applies to the following individuals on the medical plan:

  • Team member who indicates tobacco use;
  • Spouse/domestic partner who indicates tobacco use; and
  • Any dependents age 18+ who indicate they are tobacco users, regardless of how many dependents are tobacco users.
Can I change my tobacco-use status?

You can change your tobacco-use status once you are tobacco-free for six months or you complete the Quit for Life program outlined below.

What is Target doing to help me and my family stop using tobacco?

Target offers free access to the Quit For Life program for all team members and medically enrolled dependents. The program provides individualized phone-based coaching and online support to help tobacco users quit.
